RECOMMENDATIONS BASED ON OUR POLICY

Note: Based on OUR POLICY with analysis and findings, we recommend the following actions .


1. Strengthen Wi-Fi Network Security:

    - Upgrade encryption algorithms from WEP or WPA, WPA2 to the latest standard, WPA3, to ensure stronger protection against unauthorized access.

    - Implement a robust password policy requiring complex, unique passwords for Wi-Fi access. Consider using passphrases for added security.

    - Enable Wi-Fi Protected Access (WPA) Enterprise mode for authentication, which offers more secure authentication than WPA Personal mode.

    - Deploy intrusion detection and prevention systems (IDPS) to monitor network traffic for suspicious activities and potential security breaches.

    - References: https://www.cnet.com/home/internet/stop-home-network-hackers-top-10-tips-to-protect-your-wi-fi-security


2. Secure the Router

    - Disable unnecessary services and close unused ports on the router to minimize the attack surface and reduce the risk of exploitation.

    - Change default administrative credentials (username and password) to strong, unique values. Use a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.

    - Regularly update the router firmware to patch known vulnerabilities and ensure that the latest security updates are applied.

    - Implement strong access control policies to restrict administrative access to the router, allowing only authorized personnel to make configuration changes.

    - References: https://www.linkedin.com/advice/1/what-best-practices-securing-your-router-skills-technical-support-rfisc


3. Conduct Security Awareness Training:

    - Provide comprehensive security awareness training to all employees to educate them about the importance of cybersecurity and best practices for safeguarding sensitive information.

    - Offer phishing awareness training to help employees recognize and report suspicious emails and phishing attempts.

    - Conduct regular security drills and simulations to test employees'responses to security incidents and reinforce training.

    - References: https://aware.eccouncil.org/security-awareness-training-6-important-training-practices.html


4. Implement Regular Security Audits:

    - Schedule periodic security assessments and audits to identify vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, and weaknesses in the network infrastructure.

    - Perform penetration testing and vulnerability scanning to simulate real-world attacks and evaluate the effectiveness of existing security controls.

    - Establish a formal incident response plan outlining procedures for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security incidents.

    - References: https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/how-conduct-regular-security-audits-safety-ops-specialists/


5. Follow Industry Standards and Guidelines:

    - Align security practices with industry standards and best practices, such as the NIST Cybersecurity Framework, CIS Controls, and ISO/IEC 27001.

    - Stay informed about emerging threats and security trends by participating in industry forums, conferences, and information-sharing communities.

    - Engage with cybersecurity professionals and consultants to assess security posture, address compliance requirements, and implement risk management strategies

    - References: https://fastercapital.com/content/Industry-Standards--How-to-Learn-and-Follow-the-Industry-Standards-for-Your-Technical-and-Occupational-Field.html
